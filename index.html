Tour Guide Autonomous Agent

Project Description

Meet CoCoGo!

Campus tours are a very important part of the admission process at Connecticut College, playing an important role in attracting applicants and decreasing the school’s acceptance rate. In order to modernize this ancient tradition, we developed an autonomous agent called CoCoGo that is able to lead parts of campus tours. Equipped with two plates of quarter-inch aluminum, heavy duty power tools, and a lot of persistence, we successfully built CoCoGo's body from scratch. At first, we planned on using either an Arduino or a Raspberry Pi as the controller. However, we loved both of them so much that we couldn't pick just one, so we ended up using two Arduinos and one Raspberry Pi. Then, through a lot of wiring, re-wiring, coding, and just a little bit of smoke, we programmed CoCo so that while it moves around campus it is able to uses GPS data to give information on the buildings it passes. CoCo also uses ping sensors to avoid both human and non-human obstacles so that visitors’ shins and the beautiful trees on campus are safe. While on a tour, CoCo sets a nice leisurely pace so that visitors are able to take in all the scenic sites on campus. Visitors will be amazed by CoCo’s bubbling personality and her life-like face and mannerisms!
